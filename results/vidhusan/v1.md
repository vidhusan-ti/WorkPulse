**Prompt:**
> An idea to play with: sometimes the best way to learn is to feel the pain to learn what is painful. what if there was a roleplay section where the AI played the role of a vague, complex thinking, unable to create mental clarity person who can take any situation and make it more complex, person who can take a vague problem statement and make it more vague, person who can keep talking but produces no insight and forward movement. And the human has to a) deal with it and try to solve a problem together, b) at the end of it provide feedback and coaching. This shouldn't come at the end, it should be in the middle, so that it creates appreciation for what the [good communication] sections teach.

**Why above bar:** Vidhusan inverts the typical coaching model — instead of the AI as teacher, he proposes the AI as deliberately bad communicator to create experiential learning. The insight (put it in the middle, not at the end, to create appreciation for what follows) is a pedagogical design decision the LLM would not have generated; it reflects real understanding of how contrast-based learning works.
