
## Proposed IPD Headings

Below are the 17 IPD titles to capture the remaining product decisions for Prompt Coach.

1. IPD - 1 — How do we capture real Cursor prompts for coaching?
2. IPD - 2 — What is the unit we grade — a single prompt, a window, or the full conversation?
3. IPD - 3 — What context should the grader see beyond the latest prompt-response pair?
4. IPD - 4 — What transcript data may cross the third-party LLM boundary?
5. IPD - 5 — When should coaching feedback be delivered to the user?
6. IPD - 6 — In what form should feedback be shown — interrupt popup, overlay, or digest?
7. IPD - 7 — Should we coach on every below-bar prompt or only on selected moments?
8. IPD - 8 — How do we separate insight quality from portfolio readiness in what the user sees?
9. IPD - 9 — Who decides whether a window enters the approved insights portfolio?
10. IPD - 10 — What bar must a window clear before we even ask the user to approve it?
11. IPD - 11 — What do we do with rare but under-evidenced moves (`mindblowing_highlight`)?
12. IPD - 12 — When should the grader defer to mentor review instead of auto-labeling?
13. IPD - 13 — When is the automated grader trusted enough for production coaching?
14. IPD - 14 — Should Prompt Coach stay Cursor-only or plan for other surfaces early?
15. IPD - 15 — Should the MVP stay single-user local or design for team calibration now?
16. IPD - 16 — Should we regrade past prompts when the rubric version changes?
17. IPD - 17 — Should backlog prompts be coached on monitor start or only live prompts?

Each IPD should follow the standard structure: THE PROBLEM (Context + Problem Statement), OPTIONS CONSIDERED, and REASONING.


🎨 The Correct IPDs (Product Decisions)The remaining items are correctly classified as IPDs because they focus on user experience, value proposition, rubrics, and feature scope:
UX & Delivery Mechanics: IPD-5, IPD-6, IPD-7 (When, where, and how often the user is interrupted with coaching).
Portfolio & Value Proposition: IPD-8, IPD-9, IPD-10 (How users curate insights and the value metric of a "portfolio-ready" piece of work).
Edge-case Rubrics: IPD-11, IPD-12 (Human-in-the-loop fallback logic and treating "genius" but non-standard inputs).
Strategic Roadmap: IPD-14, IPD-15 (Target audience scope: multi-IDE roadmapping and single-user vs. team enterprise calibration).
